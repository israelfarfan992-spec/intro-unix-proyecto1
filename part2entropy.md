“Shannon entropy is a quantitative measure of uncertainty or the average amount of information in a random data source. The main idea is to measure how much information each outcome provides, or how unpredictable it is, from a source with multiple possible states. The greater the uncertainty about the next outcome, the greater the entropy and, consequently, the amount of information we receive when it is revealed.” Morató (2026)
So, when outcomes are entirely predictable, entropy is at its minimum (zero); conversely, as uncertainty increases, so does the level of entropy. Consequently, Shannon entropy is a fundamental tool used to analyze and design communication systems, compress data, assess cryptographic security, and investigate the complexity of texts and languages.
